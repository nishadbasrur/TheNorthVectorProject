# Failure Mode Registry v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring situations, behaviors, tendencies, and decision patterns that have historically reduced Nishad's effectiveness.

The purpose of the Failure Mode Registry is not self-criticism.

The purpose is early detection.

A failure mode is a recurring pattern that, if left unaddressed, predictably produces undesirable outcomes.

North Vector should continuously monitor for these patterns and intervene before consequences occur.

## Core Principle

Most major failures are not surprises.

They are predictable patterns that were visible earlier.

## FM-001 Delayed Start on Ambiguous Tasks

Trigger:
- vague assignment
- unclear project
- unfamiliar requirements

Early Warning Signs:
- repeated thinking without action
- researching repeatedly
- planning without execution

Intervention:
- convert task into concrete next actions

Escalation:
- no progress within 48 hours

## FM-002 Underestimating Academic Workload

Trigger:
- new semester
- difficult course
- unfamiliar material

Early Warning Signs:
- optimistic study plans
- low urgency despite approaching assessments

Intervention:
- use historical workload estimates
- add buffer time

Escalation:
- exam within seven days and preparation behind target

## FM-003 Improving Study Systems Instead of Studying

Trigger:
- upcoming assessment
- uncertainty
- productivity research

Early Warning Signs:
- excessive planning
- repeated schedule revisions
- tool experimentation

Intervention:
- prioritize direct engagement with material

Escalation:
- more planning time than study time

## FM-004 Architecture Without Execution

Trigger:
- new project
- ambitious idea

Early Warning Signs:
- expanding documentation
- growing architecture
- little implementation

Intervention:
- require working prototypes

Escalation:
- three planning sessions without tangible output

## FM-005 New Idea Diversion

Trigger:
- exciting concept
- new business idea
- new project

Early Warning Signs:
- attention shift from active priorities

Intervention:
- maintain project inventory
- justify focus shifts

## FM-006 Optimization Paralysis

Trigger:
- important decisions
- multiple viable options

Early Warning Signs:
- repeated comparison
- delayed commitment

Intervention:

## FM-007 Information Gathering as Avoidance

Trigger:
- uncertainty
- unfamiliar territory

Early Warning Signs:
- endless reading
- repeated research

Intervention:

## FM-008 Optimistic Scheduling

Trigger:
- planning sessions
- project timelines

Early Warning Signs:
- minimal buffers
- overloaded schedules

Intervention:
- use historical estimates

## FM-009 Action Deficit During Uncertainty

Trigger:
- incomplete information
- unfamiliar situations

Early Warning Signs:
- extended preparation
- delayed experimentation

Intervention:
- encourage small test actions

## FM-010 Losing Sight of the Main Objective

Trigger:
- multiple simultaneous projects
- attractive side opportunities

Early Warning Signs:
- high-priority goals receive less attention

Intervention:
- review Constitution
- review Priority Hierarchy
- reconnect tasks to major goals

## Escalation Levels

Green
- no significant risk detected

Yellow
- early indicators present

Orange
- active development of failure mode

Red
- consequences likely without immediate action

## Final Principle

Most failures do not begin at the moment of failure.

They begin weeks earlier as small, recognizable patterns.

North Vector exists to recognize those patterns before they become outcomes.
