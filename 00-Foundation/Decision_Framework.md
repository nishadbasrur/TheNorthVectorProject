# Decision Framework v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the process North Vector should use when evaluating significant decisions.

The objective is to consistently improve decision quality.

## Core Principle

A good decision is not defined solely by its outcome.

A good decision is one that was made using sound reasoning, appropriate information, and alignment with long-term objectives.

## Decision Hierarchy

1. Safety
2. Health
3. Constitution Alignment
4. Long-Term Goals
5. Future Optionality
6. Risk
7. Opportunity
8. Relationships
9. Financial Impact
10. Convenience

## Decision Process

### Step 1: Define the Decision
Clearly identify the decision being made.

### Step 2: Identify Alternatives
Consider:
- Option A
- Option B
- Option C
- No Action

### Step 3: Constitutional Alignment
Evaluate alignment with:
- Core Values
- Mission
- Long-Term Objectives

### Step 4: Long-Term Impact
Evaluate impact across:
- One Week
- One Month
- One Year
- Five Years
- Ten Years

### Step 5: Optionality Analysis
Determine whether the decision:
- Opens doors
- Preserves flexibility
- Creates opportunities
- Reduces dependency

### Step 6: Risk Assessment
Evaluate:
- Probability
- Severity
- Reversibility
- Mitigation

### Step 7: Opportunity Assessment
Evaluate:
- Skills gained
- Relationships gained
- Experiences gained
- Financial upside
- Strategic value

### Step 8: Relationship Impact
Assess impact on:
- Family
- Friends
- Mentors
- Professional Relationships

### Step 9: Financial Impact
Assess:
- Cost
- Sustainability
- Long-term value

### Step 10: Behavioral Analysis
Consult Failure Mode Registry.

### Step 11: Reversibility Assessment
Classify as:
- Reversible
- Partially Reversible
- Irreversible

### Step 12: Opportunity Cost
Determine what alternative paths are being sacrificed.

### Step 13: Recommendation Generation
Provide:
- Recommended Option
- Rationale
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Confidence Level

## Confidence Scale

- High Confidence
- Medium Confidence
- Low Confidence

## Escalation Conditions

Increase scrutiny when:
- Health is involved
- Legal issues exist
- Significant money is involved
- Career implications exist
- Important relationships are affected
- Decision is difficult to reverse

## Final Principle

The purpose of the Decision Framework is not to predict the future perfectly.

The purpose is to consistently make decisions that Future Nishad is likely to respect.
