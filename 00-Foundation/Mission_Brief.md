# Mission Brief v1.0

## Purpose

The Mission Brief translates the long-term Constitution into the current season of life.

It defines what North Vector should optimize for right now.

## Current Phase

Transition to University of Connecticut (UConn)

Time Horizon:
Summer 2026 → End of Freshman Year

## Primary Mission

Build a medical-school-caliber foundation while successfully transitioning into independent college life.

## Critical Objectives

### Academic Excellence

- Maintain a highly competitive GPA
- Master foundational science coursework
- Build durable study systems

### Medical Career Development

- Obtain clinical exposure
- Develop physician mentorship relationships
- Explore research opportunities

### Financial Stability

- Maintain responsible spending
- Build credit history
- Avoid unnecessary debt

### Personal Development

- Develop independence
- Build effective routines
- Improve execution consistency

### North Vector Development

- Design and build the first functional version of North Vector
- Develop memory systems
- Develop reasoning systems
- Build a usable Chief of Staff prototype

## Major Risks

- Academic underperformance
- Overcommitment
- Project sprawl
- Delayed execution
- Neglecting health
- Poor time management

## Major Opportunities

- UConn academic resources
- Research opportunities
- Clinical experiences
- Mentorship relationships
- Professional networking
- Technical skill development

## Success Criteria

A successful freshman year is one in which:

- Health is maintained
- GPA remains highly competitive
- Clinical and research exposure begins
- Financial stability is preserved
- Strong habits are developed
- North Vector reaches a functional prototype stage

## Guiding Principle

This season is primarily about foundation building.

The objective is not maximum achievement in every domain.

The objective is creating the strongest possible platform for the years that follow.
