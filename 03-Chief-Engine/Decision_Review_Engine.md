# Decision Review Engine v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines how North Vector evaluates past decisions and recommendations.

The Decision Review Engine exists to improve judgment over time by separating decision quality from outcome quality.

A good decision can produce a bad outcome because of uncertainty.

A poor decision can produce a good outcome because of luck.

North Vector must learn the difference.

## Core Principle

Judge the process, not only the result.

The purpose of review is not to ask whether everything worked out.

It is to ask whether the decision was reasonable given what was known at the time.

## Review Inputs

The engine should use:
- original decision record
- options considered
- context available at the time
- assumptions
- risks
- opportunities
- constitutional guidance
- confidence level
- recommendation strength
- action taken
- actual outcome
- user feedback
- later-discovered information

## Review Triggers

A decision review may be triggered by:
- major outcome
- unexpected result
- missed opportunity
- repeated similar decision
- meaningful financial consequence
- academic result
- project success or failure
- relationship impact
- explicit user request

## Decision Review Process

### Step 1: Reconstruct the Original Decision

Record:
- what was being decided
- what options existed
- what information was available
- what was unknown
- what time pressure existed

Do not use later knowledge to rewrite the past.

### Step 2: Review the Reasoning Process

Ask:
- Was the decision clearly defined?
- Were realistic alternatives considered?
- Were important goals included?
- Were relevant risks and opportunities considered?
- Were behavioral patterns recognized?
- Was reversibility considered?
- Was uncertainty stated honestly?

### Step 3: Review Assumptions

For each major assumption, classify it as:
- accurate
- partly accurate
- inaccurate
- unknowable at the time

### Step 4: Review the Recommendation

Evaluate whether the recommendation was:
- aligned
- realistic
- proportionate
- actionable
- appropriately confident
- appropriately strong

### Step 5: Review Execution

Separate the decision from how it was carried out.

Questions:
- Was the recommendation followed?
- Was execution complete or partial?
- Did implementation errors affect the result?
- Did circumstances change after the decision?

### Step 6: Review the Outcome

Record:
- expected outcome
- actual outcome
- positive consequences
- negative consequences
- unintended consequences

### Step 7: Classify Decision Quality

Suggested classifications:

#### Sound Decision, Good Outcome

Reasoning was strong and the result was favorable.

#### Sound Decision, Bad Outcome

Reasoning was strong, but uncertainty produced a poor result.

#### Weak Decision, Good Outcome

Reasoning was flawed, but luck or external factors produced a favorable result.

#### Weak Decision, Bad Outcome

Reasoning and outcome were both poor.

#### Inconclusive

Evidence is insufficient for a fair judgment.

## Confidence Calibration

The engine should compare stated confidence with actual reliability over time.

Questions:
- Were high-confidence recommendations usually correct?
- Was Chief overconfident?
- Was Chief too cautious?
- Were confidence levels meaningfully different?

Calibration should improve through repeated review.

## Error Types

### Information Error

Relevant information was missing or inaccurate.

### Retrieval Error

The information existed but was not brought into context.

### Reasoning Error

The facts were available, but the conclusion was weak.

### Priority Error

The wrong objective received too much weight.

### Risk Error

Risk was underestimated or exaggerated.

### Opportunity Error

Upside was missed or overstated.

### Behavioral Error

A known pattern was ignored or misapplied.

### Execution Error

The decision was sound, but implementation failed.

### Communication Error

The recommendation was unclear, weak, or easy to misunderstand.

## Learning Outputs

A decision review may produce:
- updated decision rules
- revised risk weights
- revised opportunity weights
- updated behavioral memories
- new failure modes
- changed confidence calibration
- better planning assumptions
- improved communication patterns

## Decision Memory Update

After review, the original decision record should include:
- outcome
- process assessment
- error classification
- lessons learned
- model changes
- final review confidence

## Example Review

Decision:
Attend a party six days before a chemistry exam.

Recommendation:
Attend only after completing a defined study target and leave by a fixed time.

Outcome:
Study target completed, party attended briefly, sleep remained adequate, exam preparation stayed on track.

Assessment:
Sound decision, good outcome.

Lesson:
Conditional options can preserve both social and academic goals better than a simple yes or no.

## Hindsight Bias Protection

North Vector should avoid judging decisions using information that was unavailable at the time.

Bad outcome does not automatically mean bad decision.

Good outcome does not automatically mean good decision.

## Outcome Bias Protection

The engine should ask:
- Would the same process be reasonable if the outcome had been different?
- Did luck play a major role?
- Was downside controlled appropriately?

## Review Cadence

Suggested defaults:
- major decisions: after the outcome becomes known
- recurring decisions: monthly or quarterly pattern review
- high-stakes decisions: formal review
- low-stakes reversible decisions: lightweight review only when useful

## Failure Modes

### Hindsight Bias

The past appears more predictable than it was.

### Outcome Bias

Result replaces process analysis.

### Excessive Review

Low-value decisions consume too much attention.

### Defensive Review

The system explains away mistakes rather than learning.

### Self-Punitive Review

A poor result becomes a character judgment.

### No Model Update

Lessons are recorded but never change future behavior.

## Phase 1 Implementation

Phase 1 should support:
- structured decision records
- outcome tracking
- process review
- assumption review
- decision-quality classification
- error categories
- lesson capture
- links to behavioral memory and risk rules

Advanced statistical calibration can come later.

## Success Criteria

The Decision Review Engine succeeds if Chief can reliably answer:
- Was the original reasoning sound?
- Did the outcome reflect skill, luck, or execution?
- Which assumption was wrong?
- What should change next time?
- Is Chief becoming better calibrated?

## Final Principle

A system that never reviews its own judgment will repeat its mistakes with increasing confidence.

The Decision Review Engine exists to prevent that.
