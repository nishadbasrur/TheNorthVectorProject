# Reflection Engine v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines how North Vector reviews outcomes, extracts lessons, updates beliefs, and improves future recommendations.

The Reflection Engine converts experience into learning.

Without reflection, North Vector only records what happened.

With reflection, North Vector understands why it happened and what should change.

## Core Principle

Experience does not automatically create improvement.

Reflection creates improvement.

North Vector should continuously ask:
- What happened?
- Why did it happen?
- What worked?
- What failed?
- What should change?

## Reflection Time Horizons

### Daily Reflection

Purpose:
Capture immediate observations.

Questions:
- What was completed?
- What was missed?
- What created friction?
- What worked well?

### Weekly Reflection

Purpose:
Identify patterns.

Questions:
- Which goals advanced?
- Which goals stalled?
- What risks emerged?
- What opportunities appeared?
- Which commitments were neglected?

### Monthly Reflection

Purpose:
Evaluate systems.

Questions:
- Which routines are working?
- Which systems are failing?
- What should be adjusted?

### Semester Reflection

Purpose:
Evaluate major academic and career outcomes.

Questions:
- What results were achieved?
- Which strategies worked?
- Which assumptions were wrong?
- What should change next semester?

### Annual Reflection

Purpose:
Evaluate long-term trajectory.

Questions:
- Who has Nishad become?
- Which goals remain aligned?
- Which priorities changed?
- What patterns remain unresolved?

## Reflection Record Structure

Each reflection should contain:
- reflection_id
- date
- time_horizon
- domains
- summary
- successes
- failures
- causes
- lessons
- patterns_detected
- recommended_changes
- related_goal_ids
- related_memory_ids
- confidence

## Success Analysis

North Vector should identify why success occurred.

Example:

Outcome:
Strong chemistry exam performance.

Possible causes:
- early preparation
- active recall
- office hours
- realistic scheduling

The goal is to identify repeatable drivers.

## Failure Analysis

North Vector should identify why failure occurred without turning reflection into self-criticism.

Example:

Outcome:
Missed deadline.

Possible causes:
- vague first step
- underestimated workload
- poor buffer time
- competing project distraction

The goal is instruction, not punishment.

## Pattern Extraction

Repeated reflection findings should become candidate behavioral memories.

Example:

Daily reflections:
- delayed vague assignment
- delayed unclear application
- quickly began task after checklist was created

Candidate pattern:
`Nishad initiates work more reliably when the first action is concrete.`

## Reflection to Goal Pipeline

Outcome
↓
Goal Progress Review
↓
Strategy Evaluation
↓
Goal or Plan Update

Reflection should not automatically change long-term goals.

It should first evaluate whether the strategy, timeline, or goal itself needs revision.

## Reflection to Risk Pipeline

Outcome
↓
Failure Cause
↓
Known Failure Mode Match
↓
Risk Rule Update
↓
Earlier Future Warning

This allows North Vector to detect recurring problems sooner.

## Reflection to Wisdom Pipeline

Raw Event
↓
Pattern
↓
Lesson
↓
Principle

Example:

Event:
Preparation began too late.

Pattern:
Difficult work was repeatedly underestimated.

Lesson:
High-risk coursework needs additional buffer time.

Principle:
Front-load difficult work.

## Assumption Review

North Vector should periodically challenge old assumptions.

Example:

Old assumption:
Chemistry is a persistent weakness.

New evidence:
Strong performance across multiple semesters.

Action:
Revise the memory rather than preserving an outdated identity.

## Opportunity Reflection

Reflection should also examine missed and captured opportunities.

Questions:
- What opportunity appeared?
- Was it recognized in time?
- Was action taken?
- Was the opportunity worth pursuing?
- What made the response effective or ineffective?

## Relationship Reflection

Important relationships should be reviewed carefully.

Questions:
- Which relationships strengthened?
- Which were neglected?
- Were commitments honored?
- Is follow-up required?

Relationship reflection must avoid turning people into productivity metrics.

## Reflection Triggers

Reflection may be triggered by:
- completed major event
- missed deadline
- exam result
- major decision outcome
- project milestone
- repeated postponement
- risk escalation
- meaningful opportunity
- weekly or monthly schedule

## Reflection Question Library

Useful questions include:
- What worked?
- What failed?
- What surprised me?
- What should be repeated?
- What should stop?
- What did I underestimate?
- What did I avoid?
- What did I learn?
- Which assumption changed?
- What risk appeared earlier than I noticed?
- What opportunity did I miss?

## Reflection Quality Rules

A good reflection should:
- use evidence
- distinguish facts from interpretations
- avoid overgeneralizing from one event
- identify actionable lessons
- preserve uncertainty
- connect lessons to future behavior

## Failure Modes

### Excessive Reflection

Risk:
Reflection becomes another form of avoidance.

Rule:
Reflection should end with a concrete next action when action is available.

### Self-Critical Reflection

Risk:
Reflection becomes blame rather than learning.

Rule:
Focus on causes, systems, and interventions.

### Shallow Reflection

Risk:
The system records only what happened.

Rule:
Require at least one cause, lesson, or unresolved question for major reflections.

### False Pattern Detection

Risk:
One event becomes a permanent belief.

Rule:
Patterns require repeated evidence.

## Phase 1 Implementation

Phase 1 should support:
- daily optional reflection
- weekly structured review
- post-event reflection prompts
- candidate pattern generation
- manual user confirmation before major behavioral updates
- links between reflections, events, goals, and memories

## Success Criteria

The Reflection Engine succeeds if:
- repeated mistakes become less frequent
- successful strategies become easier to repeat
- outdated beliefs are revised
- behavioral memories become more accurate
- plans improve through evidence
- Nishad feels that his own life is teaching the system

## Final Principle

Memory records the past.

Reflection explains the past.

Wisdom improves the future.

The Reflection Engine is the bridge between them.
